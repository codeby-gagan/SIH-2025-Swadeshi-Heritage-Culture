Exhibit of the Month

The Great Stupa at Sanchi

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1989, the Great Stupa at Sanchi stands as a testament to the enduring legacy of Buddhist art and architecture in India. Originally commissioned by the Mauryan emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE, this monumental structure has witnessed centuries of additions and renovations, each layer adding to its rich historical narrative.

Explore the intricate carvings of its toranas (gateways) which depict scenes from the life of the Buddha and the Jataka tales, offering a window into the spiritual and cultural milieu of ancient India.
