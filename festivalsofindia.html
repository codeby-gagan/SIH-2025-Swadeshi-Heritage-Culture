Festivals of India

Experience the vibrant colors, joyful music, and rich traditions of India's myriad festivals.

Diwali

October/November

The festival of lights, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. Homes are decorated with lamps and rangoli.

Key Rituals: Lighting diyas, Lakshmi Puja, exchanging gifts.

Holi

March

The festival of colors, celebrating the arrival of spring and love. People playfully throw colored powder and water.

Key Rituals: Holika Dahan, playing with colors, sharing sweets.

Durga Puja

September/October

A ten-day festival celebrating the goddess Durga's victory over the demon Mahishasur. Marked by elaborate pandals.

Key Rituals: Pandal hopping, cultural performances, immersion of idols.

Onam

August/September

Kerala's harvest festival, celebrating the homecoming of King Mahabali. Features a grand feast called Onam Sadhya.

Key Rituals: Pookkalam (floral carpet), Vallam Kali (boat race), Onam Sadhya.
